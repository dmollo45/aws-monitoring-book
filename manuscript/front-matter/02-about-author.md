# About the Author

**David Mollo** is a data infrastructure engineer and AWS practitioner based in Dublin, Ireland, specializing in serverless architecture, infrastructure monitoring, and ETL data pipeline development.


## Education

David holds a **Master's degree in Computer Science with a specialization in Big Data** from Griffith College Dublin. His academic background in distributed systems, data processing, and cloud computing provides the theoretical foundation for the practical implementations documented in this book.

His graduate studies focused on large-scale data processing, distributed architectures, and modern cloud technologies—knowledge that directly informed the design decisions and optimization strategies used in the infrastructure monitoring pipeline.

## Professional Background

David's journey into cloud engineering began with a passion for building scalable, reliable systems that solve real-world problems. Combining his academic expertise in Big Data with hands-on AWS experience, he has developed deep knowledge in serverless technologies, event-driven architectures, and observability solutions.

Currently working at at Amazon Data Services (AWS) in Dublin, David brings practical experience from mission-critical data center environments to his cloud engineering projects. His hands-on approach to learning—building complete projects from scratch, documenting every step, and sharing knowledge has shaped his understanding of cloud infrastructure and best practices.

## The Home Lab Journey

This book emerged from David's home lab in Dublin, where he dedicates time to hands-on experimentation with AWS services. The 15-day project documented in these pages represents his commitment to deep learning not just following tutorials, but building complete, production-ready systems from first principles.

His home lab serves as a testing ground for architectural patterns, optimization techniques, and best practices that he later applies to real-world projects. The infrastructure monitoring pipeline documented in this book is a testament to this approach: starting with a clear goal, working through challenges systematically, and documenting every lesson learned along the way.

## Philosophy on Learning and Sharing

David believes that the best way to learn cloud technologies is through hands-on implementation. Theory provides foundation, but building real systems—with all their challenges, failures, and breakthroughs—creates lasting understanding.

This philosophy drives his approach to documentation. Rather than presenting polished, perfect solutions, he shares the complete journey: the initial designs, the problems encountered, the solutions attempted, and the lessons learned. This honest, comprehensive approach helps others avoid common pitfalls and accelerate their own learning.

## Why This Book

After completing the 15-day infrastructure monitoring pipeline project in February 2026, David realized he had created something valuable: a complete, documented journey from zero to production-grade observability. The detailed phase-by-phase documentation, comprehensive screenshots, and real code examples represented exactly the resource he wished had existed when he started.

This book combines his academic background in Big Data and Computer Science with practical AWS implementation experience, SRE principles, and software development best practices—providing readers with both theoretical understanding and hands-on guidance.

## Beyond This Book

David continues to explore AWS services, build new projects in his home lab, and share knowledge through documentation and code repositories. His GitHub repositories (https://github.com/dmollo45/infra-monitoring-pipeline) contain the complete code for this project and other AWS implementations.
